MyHealthOne clinical portal launch at TriStar Nashville

The MyHealthOne clinical portal recently launched in the TriStar Nashville area. This launch represented an important milestone for the many people across HCA who have spent the last two years conceptualizing, designing, developing and marketing the portal. I had a chance to sit down with HCAâ€™s director of portal services, JR Allen, to chat about the launch and what we can expect to see in the future with the portal.

JR Allen
HCA Director of Portal Services

Tell us a little about the MyHealthOne clinical portal that just launched in TriStar Nashville.

We launched MHO in August in the TriStar Divisionâ€™s Nashville-area facilities. This launch marks the first time our patients can access their clinical data in an online portal. Last December, we launched the consumer portal, which allows patients to use the online scheduling tool to book appointments or register for classes and events. MyHealthOne is a single access point to all HCA information for our patients, and will replace the MEDITECH patient portal (which is currently the electronic record system of most of our hospitals).

What was one of the biggest challenges you had to overcome with this product launch?

We encountered quite a few challenges with this product launch, but one of the big ones that comes to mind is figuring out how we were going to gather all the clinical information necessary to meet Meaningful Use goals. It took several teams throughout HCA, and more than eight different departments within IT&S, to make this work. When you have this many people working on such a large launch, it can be difficult to get everyone to agree on things and solve the really complex issues. But in the end, we were able to work together to not only hit the deadline, but create this amazing portal that will help change the face of healthcare and the experience for our patients and consumers.

What can we expect to see in Phase 2?

In November, we will go live in Denver. At the same time, we will roll out a few new things such as online pre-procedure forms, which will capture the medical history of the patient and pre-populate demographics that are already in the system.
